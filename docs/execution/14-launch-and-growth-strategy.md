# Launch and Growth Strategy

## Launch principle

Launch dense, not broad.

## Best initial market shapes
- Tucson only
- Phoenix only
- brewery food truck circuit
- office park lunch trucks
- nightlife trucks
- event/catering focused trucks

## Supply-side strategy first

This product needs truck participation first.

### Manual onboarding steps
- personally reach out to 10 to 20 trucks
- create or help create profiles
- help upload photos and menus
- help owners understand the go-live flow
- gather friction feedback immediately

## Demand-side strategy second

Once enough supply exists, market to:
- local foodies
- brewery patrons
- apartment communities
- office lunch crowds
- community event audiences
- event hosts

## Early validation questions for owners
- how do customers find you now
- what causes missed customers
- how often do you post updates
- what is frustrating about current workflow
- what would make this worth paying for

## Early validation questions for customers
- how do you find food trucks now
- do you trust posted schedules
- have you shown up and found no truck
- would you follow favorite trucks for reliable alerts
- what matters more: distance, cuisine, menu, or trust

## Monetization starting point

Charge owners first, not customers.

### Free owner tier
- basic profile
- basic listing
- simple menu
- limited schedule / live tools

### Pro owner tier
- unlimited live updates
- enhanced analytics
- multiple upcoming stops
- booking tools
- promoted placement
- follower alerts later

### Host monetization later
- booking fees
- premium coordination tools

## Growth loop

1. More trucks update
2. More customers trust the platform
3. More customers follow and use it
4. Owners see more value
5. More owners participate
6. The data gets denser
7. The platform becomes more useful

## Key risk to manage

If the map feels empty, the product feels fake.  
That is why city density matters more than broad coverage.
