# Brand / Product Positioning Doc

## Product category

A **live food truck discovery and operations platform**.

## Strong product framing

This product should be positioned as:

> **The live operating system for food trucks**

That is stronger than:
- a food truck finder
- a truck directory
- a restaurant map clone

## Core positioning statement

For customers, it helps people find food trucks live nearby with more confidence.  
For truck owners, it helps them stay visible, update fast, and turn movement into revenue.  
For hosts, it makes booking trucks less chaotic.

## One-line positioning options

- **Find food trucks live. Run your truck smarter.**
- **Live discovery for hungry customers. Simple ops for food trucks.**
- **Real-time food truck discovery and operations.**
- **Where food trucks go live.**

## Target users

### Primary audience
Truck owners and managers who need:
- better discoverability
- easier status/location updates
- fewer missed customers
- better event coordination

### Secondary audience
Customers who want:
- fast nearby discovery
- reliable open-now information
- better alternatives to messy Instagram searching

### Tertiary audience
Hosts and venues who want:
- easier booking
- more reliable vendor coordination

## Core pains to address

### Customers
- Instagram is messy for real-time location discovery
- schedules are unreliable
- users show up and trucks are not there
- menus and hours are often stale

### Owners
- constant manual posting is annoying
- customers miss their location
- Instagram is not designed for live nearby discovery
- event and recurring stop coordination is fragmented

### Hosts
- booking is DM-and-text chaos
- reliability is hard to assess
- it is inefficient to fill event slots

## Emotional value

### For customers
- confidence
- convenience
- spontaneity
- discovery

### For owners
- control
- visibility
- professionalism
- revenue confidence

### For hosts
- clarity
- reliability
- reduced chaos

## Brand attributes

The product should feel:
- modern
- trustworthy
- alive
- location-aware
- energetic
- operational, not corporate
- polished, not cluttered

## Tone guidance

Voice should be:
- clear
- confident
- useful
- warm but efficient
- not childish
- not overly formal

## Visual direction

Think:
- map-forward
- bold food imagery
- high-legibility status chips
- clean cards
- premium but approachable
- mobile-first responsiveness

A good benchmark mix:
- Uber-level clarity
- Airbnb polish
- local street-food energy

## What the brand is not

It is not:
- a static directory
- generic restaurant discovery
- a social network first
- a heavy enterprise platform
- a coupon app

## Brand promise

### Customer-facing
Find what is nearby and live with more confidence.

### Owner-facing
Stay visible without spending your day manually posting everywhere.

### Host-facing
Book trucks with less chaos and more reliability.

## Long-term brand angle

Even if the first version is food-truck-specific, the deeper brand opportunity is broader:

> a live operations and discovery layer for mobile businesses

That said, do not dilute the initial story.  
Start tight. Win a category before expanding.

## Suggested homepage positioning copy

### Hero concept
**Find food trucks live near you**

See what's serving now, where your favorites are headed next, and which events are happening nearby.

### Owner CTA concept
**Claim your truck**
Go live in seconds, keep customers updated, and turn more stops into revenue.

### Host CTA concept
**Book food trucks for your event**
Submit requests and coordinate vendors without the usual chaos.
