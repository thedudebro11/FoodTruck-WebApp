# Product Overview

## Product definition

A **mobile-responsive web platform** for live food truck discovery and operations.

It serves three sides of the ecosystem:

1. **Customers**
   - Find nearby food trucks
   - See what is open now
   - Check menus, schedules, and live status
   - Follow favorite trucks
   - Discover events

2. **Truck owners**
   - Manage truck profile
   - Go live / go offline
   - Update current stop and public status
   - Manage menus
   - Publish upcoming schedules
   - Receive booking requests
   - Review simple business analytics

3. **Hosts / event organizers**
   - Request trucks
   - Manage vendor requests
   - Coordinate events more reliably

## Core problem

Food trucks are moving businesses. That creates a different product problem than a normal restaurant directory.

A food truck has:
- changing location
- changing hours
- changing inventory
- changing demand
- changing event participation
- frequent day-to-day variability

The real problem is not:
- "show a business listing on a map"

The real problem is:
- **help people reliably find moving food businesses, and help those businesses reliably turn movement into revenue**

## Product promise

### For customers
"I can reliably find a good food truck near me right now."

### For owners
"I can get discovered and keep customers updated without living on Instagram."

### For hosts
"I can book reliable trucks without chaos."

## Product wedge

The strongest initial wedge is:

- **real-time trust**
- **low-friction owner updates**

That means:
- customers trust what they see
- owners can publish live updates in seconds
- stale data is aggressively handled
- the platform feels operational, not just decorative

## Product loop

1. Truck goes live
2. Customers nearby discover it
3. Customers show up, save, or follow
4. Truck sees value
5. Truck keeps using the platform
6. Data stays fresh
7. Discovery quality improves
8. Repeat usage grows

## Product principles

1. **Live beats pretty**
2. **Owners are busy**
3. **Kill stale data**
4. **Discovery must be instant**
5. **Build for messy real-world operations**
6. **Charge owners before charging customers**
7. **Launch dense, not broad**

## Recommended first launch shape

Pick one city or one tight niche, such as:
- Tucson
- Phoenix
- brewery food truck circuit
- office lunch trucks
- nightlife trucks
- event/catering-focused trucks

A dense launch makes the product feel alive.  
A broad empty launch makes it feel fake.
