# Product Strategy and Mental Model

## The right mental model

This is not "Yelp for food trucks."

A dead directory fails because it ends up with:
- outdated menus
- stale hours
- wrong locations
- no reason to return

This product should be understood as:

> **A live discovery and operating system for food trucks**

## The three-sided system

### 1. Customer app
Questions customers need answered:
- what is near me right now
- what is open
- what looks good
- what is worth driving to
- what has short wait times
- where will my favorite truck be later
- what events are happening tonight

### 2. Truck owner dashboard
Questions operators need answered:
- how do I update location in seconds
- how do I publish my status fast
- how do I reuse recurring schedules
- how do I announce that I am live
- how do I get more repeat customers
- how do I handle event and catering requests

### 3. Event / host portal
Questions hosts need answered:
- how do I request trucks
- how do I know who is reliable
- how do I fill vendor slots
- how do I reduce no-shows and chaos

## What makes or breaks the product

### 1. Data quality
If truck data is stale, the whole product weakens.

### 2. Update friction
If owners have to do too much work, they stop updating.

### 3. Discovery speed
If customers cannot answer "what good truck is near me right now?" quickly, they leave.

### 4. Monetizable business value
If owners do not make more money or save time, they will not care.

## Core wedge options

### Wedge 1: Most accurate live map
- visible freshness state
- owner check-in flow
- stale expiration logic
- customer confirmations
- last updated timestamps

### Wedge 2: Best operator workflow
- one-tap go live
- repeat schedule templates
- copy yesterday's stop
- quick sold-out / busy toggles
- fast menu availability updates

### Wedge 3: Best event booking network
- request trucks
- accept / decline flow
- reliability score later
- event coordination tools

### Wedge 4: Best community and habit loop
- follow favorite trucks
- nearby alerts
- neighborhood discovery
- trending dishes
- event feeds
- loyalty later

## Key product framing

Do not describe the product as:
- "a food truck website"
- "StreetFoodFinder but better"

Describe it as:
- **a live discovery and operations platform for mobile food businesses**

## Real value hidden under the surface

The customer map is the visible hook.  
The owner operations layer may become the actual business engine.

That means long-term value may come from:
- owner workflow
- booking coordination
- recurring schedules
- trust data
- vendor network effects

## MVP proof goals

The MVP only needs to prove:

1. Truck owners will keep information updated
2. Customers will use live location/status to make decisions
3. That behavior creates real value

## What should always stay in your head

This product is about five things:
1. real-time trust
2. low-friction operator updates
3. fast customer discovery
4. repeat engagement
5. revenue for supply
